places to visit

Burj Khalifa

I have visited this place in November 2017

World's tallest building with over 100 floors! Situated in Dubai. It is really windy and cold especially at night. Burj Khalifa is an office building as well as a restuarant at the top most floor but only VIP people have access to the restuarant.


Ferrari World

I have visited this place in Nov 2017.

Ferrari World is in Abu Dabi, Dubai. It is litteraly shped in the ferrari logo! It is filled with many ferrari rides & activities such as roller coaster, remote control cars, etc. It also has ferrari models for photo taking and shops! You can buy ferrari clothes, hats, shoes, jackets, toy cars, perfumes, watches and many more!


Lego Land

I have visited this place in 2014.

Lego Land is in Malaysia. It is a resort with many activities and rides. It also has a water park! Some rooms come with a mystery riddle that will unlock the mystery safe that has some cool prizes! You can buy lots of Lego merch, sets, and school supplies!

news about environment

  • Lego plans to find replacment material for blocks by 2030.

    In an effort to be more environmentally responsible, LEGO have said they aim to stop manufacturing bricks made of plastic resin and are considering other options, which could include hemp. According to Telegraph World, the company makes 60 billion blocks a year. While LEGO want to replace the harmful plastic used in their products, they also require the new material to meet the high standards that allow the blocks to work as they always have, especially with the effective “clicking” together is a key aspect of the iconic coloured block.

    Amazon deforestation and number of fires show summer of 2019 not 'normal' year.

  • Amazon deforestation and number of fires show summer of 2019 not 'normal'.

    The perceived scale of the Amazon blazes received global attention this summer. However, international concerns raised at the time were countered by the Brazilian Government, which claimed the fire situation in August was 'normal' and 'below the historical average'. A new report finds that the number of active fires in August was actually three times higher than in 2018 and the highest number since 2010.
